The structural reasons

// not magic, just math
DENSITY
One trit holds log2(3) ≈ 1.58 bits of information. To cover the same numerical range, ternary needs ~63% as many digits.
FREE NEGATION
Subtraction in binary needs two's complement: invert all bits, add one. In balanced ternary, flip the sign of each trit. One gate per digit, in parallel, no carry chain.
ZERO TRITS SKIP
In multiplication, 1/3 of trits are zero and contribute nothing. The hardware skips them. Binary skips half its bits too, but starts with more digits.
BUT…
Distinguishing three voltage levels reliably costs ~25% per gate vs. two levels. The tradeoff is real. The net advantage is everything above, minus this.
~6× at scale.

A neural-network dot product with ternary weights (BitNet b1.58) is where the math turns brutal in ternary's favor. The "multiply" stops being a multiplier and becomes a 3-way mux: skip, add, or subtract.

As vectors get longer, the gap widens. At 1024 elements ternary uses ~6× fewer gates than binary for the same dot product. This is why the Setun's logic might come back — not as general-purpose CPUs, but as native AI accelerators.

Where binary wins
70 yrs
Manufacturing maturity, tooling, software, and economies of scale. Every benchmark above measures architecture; in the real world, this single dimension still dominates.
Where ternary wins
3.0×
Subtraction and comparison run roughly 3× cheaper. Free negation removes the entire two's-complement tax. Ternary CPUs would have simpler, smaller, lower-energy ALUs.
Where ternary destroys
~6×
Trit-weighted neural network inference. The killer app, the modern BitNet b1.58 result, and the only domain where the workload itself is shaped exactly like ternary hardware wants it to be.
