Where ternary actually wins.

96% of the advantage comes from four operations: the BitLinear projections. Everything else clusters at the generic int8-vs-fp16 ratio (~2-3×).

SCALING WITH SEQUENCE LENGTH

The advantage shrinks as context grows.

The four BitLinear projections scale as O(L · d²) where L is sequence length and d is hidden size. Attention scales as O(L² · d). So the ratio of attention to projection cost grows as L/d.

At short sequences (L=128 ≪ d=2560), BitLinear dominates and ternary wins by 9.4×. At long sequences, attention catches up; attention isn't trit-weighted (both operands are activations), so ternary loses its edge.

Implication: ternary hardware should target prefill-heavy workloads first — RAG, classification, embeddings, code completion — and a future generation of accelerators will need to invest in attention-specific optimizations to preserve the advantage at long contexts.

~6×

Headline number for purpose-built BitNet inference hardware at realistic sequence lengths. After accounting for ternary gates being ~25% slower per switch, this translates to ~3-5× area or energy advantage in real silicon — transformative for inference economics.
